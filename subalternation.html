Subalternation

According to the Ancients, Schoolmen, and Moderns

Subalternation is defined:
SaP⊃SiP
SeP⊃SoP

That is to say, if all S are P, then some S are P; if no S are P, then some S are not P. The functors a, e, i, and o correlate to the four moods of categorical syllogisms. Further, when I say "functor," I do not mean the conception devised by abstract mathematicians, rather, in the general sense of a mapping between two terms.
